Care and Healing

Mental Health
& Psychosocial Support

Why It Matters

Defenders Are Human Too

Human rights work exposes defenders to trauma, sustained fear, burnout, and emotional exhaustion. The psychological weight of this work is real — and often invisible. Semena Center prioritizes mental health not as an afterthought, but as a core protection service.

Our psychosocial support programs are trauma-informed, culturally sensitive, and built around dignity. We create spaces where defenders can process their experiences, heal, and restore the inner strength needed to continue their vital work.

"You cannot pour from an empty vessel. The wellbeing of defenders is inseparable from the wellbeing of the communities they serve."

Support Services

How We Care

Trauma-Informed Counseling

One-on-one counseling referrals with professionals trained in trauma, crisis response, and human rights work contexts.

Peer Support Networks

Structured peer communities connecting defenders who share similar experiences, building solidarity and reducing isolation.

Healing Spaces

Safe, confidential environments — physical and virtual — where defenders can rest, reflect, and receive support without judgment.

Crisis Emotional Support

Immediate emotional support during and after crisis situations, ensuring no defender processes acute trauma alone.

Wellness Awareness

Programs and training that help organizations build cultures of mental wellness and recognize signs of burnout in their teams.

Rehabilitation & Recovery

Long-term rehabilitation assistance for survivors of violence, torture, and severe trauma — restoring function and hope.

You Do Not Have to Carry This Alone

If you are a defender experiencing fear, exhaustion, trauma, or distress — reach out. Confidential support is available.
